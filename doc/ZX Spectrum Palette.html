Notes:  
  • Color number 8 Black (#00000) is displayed by some clones (specially on NTSC computers) as Dark Gray (#AAAAAA).
  • On Sinclair ZX Spectrum +128K (Toastrack) and Sinclair ZX Spectrum +2 (Gray), if connected to a CGA monitor by the RGBI connector, the displayed colors are the ones for the CGA standard palette, being the most significant changes:
        - color number 8 Black (#00000) is displayed as Dark Gray (#AAAAAA);
        - color number 6 Yellow (#AAAA00) is displayed as Brown (#AA5500);
  • Colors are order by GRBI, because that way they are order from the dark colors to the bright colors and that simplifies the conversion to gray scale on black-and-white TVs and monochrome monitors (green, amber and blue phosphor) and improves image quality;
  • The intensity bit (I), is used for both the foreground and background color on the 8x8 pixel matrix and it can't be used on the border color.
    This is due to the way the ZX Spectrum ULA and Timex SCLD (and the equivalent on the clones) work.
    The flash bit (F), timely swaps the foreground and background colors on the 8x8 pixel matrix and it can't be used on the border color.
  • The 1-byte color attribute for each character (8x8 pixels), has the following arrangement:
      Paper/Background Ink/Foreground
    F Gp2 Rp1 Bp0 I Gi2 Ri1 Bi0
    Bit: 7 6 5 4 3 2 1 0
